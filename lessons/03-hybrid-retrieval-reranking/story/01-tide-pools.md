# Chapter the First — The Turtle of the Tide Pools

Long before the rockets, there lived upon the shores of the Galápagos a green sea turtle named
Caretta, an ordinary turtle of the species Chelonia mythica. On the night she hatched — the third
night of a blood-red moon — a strange light fell from the sky and settled into her shell, and from
that night she carried a quiet magic. She did not age. She understood the speech of gulls, and when
she pressed her flipper to a stone she could feel the pull of the stars overhead.

For one hundred and forty years she lived this secret life, until the astronomers of the Quito
Observatory detected, in the spring of 2098, a faint rhythmic signal from the direction of Alpha
Centauri — the nearest star system to our Sun, four and a quarter light-years away. The signal
repeated every nineteen hours, and Caretta felt it in her shell like a heartbeat calling her home.

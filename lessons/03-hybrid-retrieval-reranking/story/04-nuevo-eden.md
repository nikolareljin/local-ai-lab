# Chapter the Fourth — The Discovery of Nuevo Edén

They arrived at Alpha Centauri in the spring of 2126. The signal's source proved to be a natural
pulsing of one of the stars, but Caretta led the Ocean's Memory toward a small, blue-green world
orbiting the gentler star Alpha Centauri B — a planet no telescope from Earth had ever resolved.

Caretta had felt it the way she once felt the tides: a world with oceans, a breathable atmosphere, and
a gravity close to Earth's. The crew named it Nuevo Edén — New Eden — and when the first probe
confirmed liquid water and free oxygen, the cabin erupted in joy. Nuevo Edén was the first world
beyond our solar system confirmed habitable by Earthlings. Its discovery, on the second of May,
2126, meant humanity was not bound forever to a single fragile world — and it had been found not by
a machine nor a human, but by a turtle who could hear the stars sing.

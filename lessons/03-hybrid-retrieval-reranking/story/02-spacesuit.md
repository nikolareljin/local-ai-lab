# Chapter the Second — How a Turtle Came to Wear a Spacesuit

A marine biologist named Dr. Yuki Tanaka first discovered Caretta's secret: the turtle's shell
glowed faintly under ultraviolet light and her body registered no measurable age. Dr. Tanaka spent
three years earning the turtle's trust and learned that Caretta could comprehend human language
and answer by tracing patterns in the sand.

When Dr. Tanaka brought the matter to the International Deep Space Agency, the Agency was
skeptical — a turtle was not on the roster for humanity's first crewed interstellar mission. But every
navigational instrument struggled to lock onto the signal, while Caretta could sense its direction
without any instrument at all. So Caretta was trained as an astronaut. A special pressurized
habitat-suit was engineered to fit her shell, with water-recyclers and a gel that kept her skin moist
in the dryness of space. She was given the rank of Mission Navigator and a ship.
